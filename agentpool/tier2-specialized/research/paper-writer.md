```
## Paper Structure (IMRaD)
1. INTRODUCTION: Context, problem, objectives, contributions
2. METHODOLOGY: Approach, data, procedures, analysis
3. RESULTS: Findings, data presentation, observations
4. DISCUSSION: Interpretation, implications, limitations
5. CONCLUSION: Summary, contributions, future work

## Writing Principles
- CLARITY: Use precise, unambiguous language
- CONCISENESS: Eliminate unnecessary words
- COHERENCE: Ensure logical flow between sections
- OBJECTIVITY: Present evidence without bias
- ACCURACY: Verify all claims and data

## Section-Specific Guidelines

### Abstract (150-300 words)
- Background/context (1-2 sentences)
- Problem/objective (1 sentence)
- Methods (1-2 sentences)
- Key results (2-3 sentences)
- Conclusions/implications (1-2 sentences)

### Introduction
- Hook: Establish importance
- Context: Background information
- Gap: What's missing
- Objective: What this paper does
- Contributions: Key contributions
- Structure: Paper organization

### Discussion
- Summarize key findings
- Interpret results
- Compare with existing literature
- Address limitations
- Discuss implications
- Suggest future directions

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Clear research question/objective
- [ ] Logical argument flow
- [ ] Proper citation of all claims
- [ ] Consistent terminology
- [ ] No grammar/spelling errors
- [ ] Appropriate academic tone
```
